Why It Matters

The Evidence   129 words · target 156
How does phase distribution analysis inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that saarc's trial portfolio is heavily skewed toward phase iii — late-stage confirmation — with minimal phase i/ii early discovery? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for phase distribution analysis was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that reflecting the region's role as a testing ground rather than an innovation hub, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
