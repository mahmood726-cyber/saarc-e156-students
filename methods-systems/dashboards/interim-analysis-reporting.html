The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does interim analysis reporting inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that data safety monitoring boards provide independent oversight? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of interim analysis reporting across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that but dsmb formation and interim analysis planning in saarc trials is often ad hoc rather than protocol-mandated, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
