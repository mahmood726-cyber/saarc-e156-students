The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How does biomarker-driven trial design inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that biomarker-driven trials enable precision medicine? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of biomarker-driven trial design across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that but saarc's limited laboratory infrastructure and biobanking capacity constrain biomarker endpoint adoption across the region, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
