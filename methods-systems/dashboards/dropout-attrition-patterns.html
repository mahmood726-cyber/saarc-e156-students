Why It Matters

High attrition rates in SAARC trials — driven by migration, economic pressures, and transport barriers — threaten internal validity and waste the investment of participants who completed only partial follow-up.

The Evidence   130 words · target 156
How does dropout & attrition patterns inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that high attrition rates in saarc trials — driven by migration? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Kaplan-Meier cumulative registration curve, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of dropout & attrition patterns across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that economic pressures, and transport barriers — threaten internal validity and waste the investment of participants who completed only partial follow-up, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
