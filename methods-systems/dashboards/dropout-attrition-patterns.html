Why It Matters

High attrition rates in SAARC trials — driven by migration, economic pressures, and transport barriers — threaten internal validity and waste the investment of participants who completed only partial follow-up.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does dropout & attrition patterns expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Kaplan-Meier cumulative registration curve as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
