Why It Matters

South Asia needs pragmatic trials that test interventions under real-world conditions with overburdened health systems, yet most SAARC trials use explanatory designs imported from well-resourced settings.

The Evidence   123 words · target 156
How does pragmatic vs explanatory inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that south asia needs pragmatic trials that test interventions under real-world conditions with overburdened health systems? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for pragmatic vs explanatory was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that yet most saarc trials use explanatory designs imported from well-resourced settings, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
