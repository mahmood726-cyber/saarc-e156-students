Why It Matters

Cross-over designs halve the required sample size, making them efficient for resource-limited settings, yet their use in SAARC is limited by high dropout rates and carryover effect concerns.

The Evidence   123 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does cross-over design usage expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
