Why It Matters

Cross-over designs halve the required sample size, making them efficient for resource-limited settings, yet their use in SAARC is limited by high dropout rates and carryover effect concerns.

The Evidence   128 words · target 156
How does cross-over design usage inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that cross-over designs halve the required sample size? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of cross-over design usage across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that making them efficient for resource-limited settings, yet their use in saarc is limited by high dropout rates and carryover effect concerns, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
