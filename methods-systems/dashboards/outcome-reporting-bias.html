The Evidence   156 words · target 156
How does outcome reporting bias inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that selective outcome reporting — publishing positive endpoints while suppressing negative ones — is a pervasive threat to evidence integrity that may be amplified in saarc requirements.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of outcome reporting bias across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that selective outcome reporting — publishing positive endpoints while suppressing negative ones — is a pervasive threat to evidence integrity that may be amplified in saarc by weak enforcement of registration requirements, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
