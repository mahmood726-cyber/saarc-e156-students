The Evidence   126 words · target 156
How does completion rate analysis inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that trial completion rates in saarc vary dramatically by country and sponsor type? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Kaplan-Meier cumulative registration curve, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of completion rate analysis across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that with early termination rates exceeding 30% in some settings — representing wasted investment and participant burden, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
