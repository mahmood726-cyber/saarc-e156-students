The Evidence   126 words · target 156
How does patient-reported outcome use inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that patient-reported outcomes capture what matters to patients? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of patient-reported outcome use across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that but most pro instruments are developed in english-speaking western populations and may lack cultural validity for south asian patients, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
