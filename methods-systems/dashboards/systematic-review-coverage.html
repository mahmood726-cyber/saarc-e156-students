Why It Matters

SAARC trials are frequently excluded from Cochrane and other systematic reviews due to perceived quality concerns, creating a vicious cycle where South Asian evidence never influences global guidelines.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
How does systematic review coverage inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that saarc trials are frequently excluded from cochrane and other systematic reviews due to perceived quality concerns? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for systematic review coverage was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that creating a vicious cycle where south asian evidence never influences global guidelines, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
