Why It Matters

Primary endpoint selection in SAARC trials reveals a reliance on surrogate markers rather than patient-important outcomes, reflecting sponsors' desire for regulatory efficiency over clinical relevance.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does primary endpoint selection expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Shannon entropy of trial allocation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index was 4164.242, exceeding the 0.25 threshold and indicating a highly concentrated regional trial portfolio. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
