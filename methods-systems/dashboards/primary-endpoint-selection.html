The Evidence   122 words · target 156
How does primary endpoint selection inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that primary endpoint selection in saarc trials reveals a reliance on surrogate markers rather than patient-important outcomes? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. The primary endpoint selection HHI was 4,164.2, exceeding the concentration threshold, with India dominating at 49%. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that reflecting sponsors' desire for regulatory efficiency over clinical relevance, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
