The Evidence   128 words · target 156
How does pilot & feasibility studies inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that pilot studies are essential for testing trial feasibility in new settings? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of pilot & feasibility studies across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that yet saarc trials often skip this step, launching full-scale trials that then fail due to predictable operational challenges, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
