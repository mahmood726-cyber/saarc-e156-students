How does network analysis of investigators inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that investigator network analysis reveals a small-world topology where a handful of well-connected pis bridge most saarc trial collaborations? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the network degree centrality, computed from registry-level metadata. The network analysis of investigators analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that creating fragility if these hub investigators retire or relocate, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
