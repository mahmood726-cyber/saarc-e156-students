Why It Matters

Investigator network analysis reveals a small-world topology where a handful of well-connected PIs bridge most SAARC trial collaborations, creating fragility if these hub investigators retire or relocate.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does network analysis of investigators expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the network degree centrality as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
