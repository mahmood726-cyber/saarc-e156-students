The Evidence   127 words · target 156
How does real-world evidence integration inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that real-world evidence from electronic health records and insurance databases is transforming research in high-income countries? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of real-world evidence integration across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that but saarc's fragmented, paper-based health systems cannot yet generate rwe at scale, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
