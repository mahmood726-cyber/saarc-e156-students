The Evidence   143 words · target 156
How does digital health trial methods inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that south asia's mobile phone penetration exceeding 70% creates unique opportunities for mhealth trials that could leapfrog traditional clinical infrastructure in the region.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of digital health trial methods across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that south asia's mobile phone penetration exceeding 70% creates unique opportunities for mhealth trials that could leapfrog traditional clinical infrastructure in the region, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
