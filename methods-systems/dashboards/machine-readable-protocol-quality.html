The Evidence   122 words · target 156
How does machine-readable protocol quality inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that machine-readable protocol fields on clinicaltrials.gov enable automated quality assessment? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for machine-readable protocol quality was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that but saarc trials have higher rates of missing, inconsistent, or free-text entries that resist automated analysis, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
