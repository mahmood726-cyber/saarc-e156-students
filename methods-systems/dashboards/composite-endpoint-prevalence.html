The Evidence   155 words · target 156
How does composite endpoint prevalence inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that composite endpoints increase statistical power but can mask clinically important differences between components — a risk when endpoints validated in western populations may not apply in south asian contexts.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. The composite endpoint prevalence HHI was 4,164.2, exceeding the concentration threshold, with India dominating at 49%. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that composite endpoints increase statistical power but can mask clinically important differences between components — a risk when endpoints validated in western populations may not apply in south asian contexts, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
