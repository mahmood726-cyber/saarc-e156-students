Why It Matters

Non-inferiority trials are critical for testing cheaper or simpler alternatives — exactly what SAARC needs — but NI margin justification is often inadequately reported in South Asian trials.

The Evidence   123 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does non-inferiority trial usage expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
