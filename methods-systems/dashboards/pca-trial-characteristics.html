Why It Matters

Principal component analysis of SAARC trial characteristics reveals latent dimensions — a pharma-commercial axis and an academic-public-health axis — that explain the majority of variation in trial design.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does pca of trial characteristics expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Spearman rank correlation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
