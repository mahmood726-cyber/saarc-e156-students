Why It Matters

Frequent protocol amendments signal poor initial planning and can introduce bias, yet SAARC trials show higher amendment rates than global averages, partly due to unrealistic enrollment projections.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does protocol amendment frequency expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
