Why It Matters

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does protocol amendment frequency inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that frequent protocol amendments signal poor initial planning and can introduce bias? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of protocol amendment frequency across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that yet saarc trials show higher amendment rates than global averages, partly due to unrealistic enrollment projections, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
