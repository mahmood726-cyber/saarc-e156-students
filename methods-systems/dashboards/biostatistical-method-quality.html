Why It Matters

Biostatistical capacity in SAARC is critically limited — South Asia has fewer trained biostatisticians than a single US academic medical center, leading to methodological errors in trial design and analysis.

The Evidence   127 words · target 156
How does biostatistical method quality inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that biostatistical capacity in saarc is critically limited — south asia has fewer trained biostatisticians than a single us academic medical center? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for biostatistical method quality was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These methodological findings indicate that leading to methodological errors in trial design and analysis, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
