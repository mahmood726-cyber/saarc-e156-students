Why It Matters

Data completeness on ClinicalTrials.gov and CTRI for SAARC trials is often poor, with missing primary outcomes, enrollment figures, and results — undermining the registry's transparency function.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does registry data quality expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
