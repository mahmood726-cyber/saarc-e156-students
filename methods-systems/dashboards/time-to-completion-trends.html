How does time-to-completion trends inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that time-to-completion for saarc trials has been increasing despite global trends toward faster trials? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of time-to-completion trends across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
