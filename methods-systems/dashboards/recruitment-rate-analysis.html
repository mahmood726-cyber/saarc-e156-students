Why It Matters

SAARC's large populations make it attractive for rapid recruitment, but actual enrollment rates vary enormously by disease, site, and country — with some trials enrolling in days and others failing entirely.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does recruitment rate analysis expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the linear regression slope as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
