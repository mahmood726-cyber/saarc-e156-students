The Evidence   125 words · target 156
How does sample size adequacy inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that underpowered trials waste resources and participants' altruism by producing inconclusive results? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of sample size adequacy across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These methodological findings indicate that yet a substantial fraction of saarc trials fail to achieve their planned enrollment targets, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
