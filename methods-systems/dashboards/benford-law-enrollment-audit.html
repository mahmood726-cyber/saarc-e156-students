The Evidence   156 words · target 156
How does benford's law enrollment audit inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that benford's law analysis of enrollment numbers can detect fabricated data — a forensic tool that reveals whether reported sample sizes follow the expected digit distribution of numbers.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of benford's law enrollment audit across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=0.031, confirming the disparity is statistically significant at the 5% level. These methodological findings indicate that benford's law analysis of enrollment numbers can detect fabricated data — a forensic tool that reveals whether reported sample sizes follow the expected digit distribution of naturally occurring numbers, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
