The Evidence   122 words · target 156
How does follow-up duration adequacy inform the methodological quality of South Asian trials, given that long-term follow-up is essential for safety monitoring and durability assessment? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Kaplan-Meier cumulative registration curve, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of follow-up duration adequacy across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These methodological findings indicate that but mobile populations and limited tracing systems in saarc make sustained follow-up exceptionally challenging, limiting evidence quality. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
