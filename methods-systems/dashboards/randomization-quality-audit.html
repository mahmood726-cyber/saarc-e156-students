Why It Matters

Proper randomization — allocation concealment and sequence generation — is the gold standard for bias prevention, but reporting of randomization methods in SAARC trials is often incomplete or absent.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does randomization quality audit expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
