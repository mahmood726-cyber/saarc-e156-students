Phase Distribution Analysis

How rigorous and methodologically sound are clinical trials conducted across South Asia, and does phase distribution analysis expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results indicate that methodological capacity gaps constrain the quality and global impact of South Asian clinical research. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
