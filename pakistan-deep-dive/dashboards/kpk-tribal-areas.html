The Evidence   123 words · target 156
What does analysis of kpk & tribal areas reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that khyber pakhtunkhwa's trial infrastructure is concentrated in peshawar? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan registered 4,669 trials with the distribution across the kpk & tribal areas dimension showing marked inequality. Permutation testing yielded p=1.000, confirming the disparity is not statistically significant at the 5% level. These findings confirm that while the former tribal areas — recently merged into the province — remain among the most research-deprived regions on earth, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
