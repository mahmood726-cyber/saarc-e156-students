Why It Matters

Provincial health budget allocation correlates with trial density, but the relationship is sublinear — doubling health spending does not double trial output, suggesting structural barriers beyond funding.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
What does analysis of provincial health budget vs trial density reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that provincial health budget allocation correlates with trial density? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the Spearman rank correlation, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that provincial health budget vs trial density quantifies. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.00 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These findings confirm that but the relationship is sublinear — doubling health spending does not double trial output, suggesting structural barriers beyond funding, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
