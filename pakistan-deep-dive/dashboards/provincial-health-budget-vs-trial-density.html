Why It Matters

Provincial health budget allocation correlates with trial density, but the relationship is sublinear — doubling health spending does not double trial output, suggesting structural barriers beyond funding.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does provincial health budget vs trial density expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Spearman rank correlation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.000) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
