Why It Matters

Endpoint selection in Pakistan trials often imports Western-validated measures without cultural adaptation, raising questions about whether outcomes measured are meaningful to Pakistani patients.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does endpoint appropriateness expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Shannon entropy of trial allocation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index was 10000.000, exceeding the 0.25 threshold and indicating a highly concentrated regional trial portfolio. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was -0.00 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
