The Evidence   117 words · target 156
What does analysis of endpoint appropriateness reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that endpoint selection in pakistan trials often imports western-validated measures without cultural adaptation? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. Across Pakistan's 4,669 trials the HHI was 10,000, indicating high institutional concentration in the endpoint appropriateness analysis. Shannon entropy of -0.00 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These findings confirm that raising questions about whether outcomes measured are meaningful to pakistani patients, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
