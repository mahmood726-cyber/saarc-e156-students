Why It Matters

Balochistan is Pakistan's largest province by area but smallest by trial count — its 14 million people have fewer clinical trials than a single hospital in Lahore, reflecting extreme infrastructure deprivation.

The Evidence   122 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does balochistan research desert expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. Pakistan registered 4,669 trials across its provinces with concentration in Punjab and Sindh dominating output. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
