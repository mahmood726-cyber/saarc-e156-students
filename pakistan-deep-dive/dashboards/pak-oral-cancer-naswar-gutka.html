Why It Matters

Pakistan has among the world's highest oral cancer rates, driven by naswar and gutka use, yet prevention and treatment trials for smokeless tobacco-related cancers are virtually nonexistent.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
Does Pakistan's Oral Cancer trial portfolio match the country's disease burden, given that pakistan has among the world's highest oral cancer rates? We audited 9 Pakistan-based Oral Cancer trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan registered 9 trials with the distribution across the oral cancer & naswar/gutka dimension showing marked inequality. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings confirm that driven by naswar and gutka use, yet prevention and treatment trials for smokeless tobacco-related cancers are virtually nonexistent, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
