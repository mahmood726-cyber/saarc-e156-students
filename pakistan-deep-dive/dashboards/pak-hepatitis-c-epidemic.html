Why It Matters

Pakistan's hepatitis C prevalence of 5-8% is among the world's highest, driven by unsafe injection practices, yet the country is reliant on generic DAA trials designed elsewhere.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
Does Pakistan's Hepatitis C trial portfolio match the country's disease burden, given that pakistan's hepatitis c prevalence of 5-8% is among the world's highest? We audited 15 Pakistan-based Hepatitis C trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan registered 15 trials with the distribution across the hepatitis c epidemic dimension showing marked inequality. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0000) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These findings confirm that driven by unsafe injection practices, yet the country is reliant on generic daa trials designed elsewhere, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
