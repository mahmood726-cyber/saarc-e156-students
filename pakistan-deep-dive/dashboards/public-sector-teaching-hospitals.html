Why It Matters

Public sector teaching hospitals like Mayo, JPMC, and Lady Reading serve the majority of Pakistan's population but host a minority of clinical trials due to infrastructure and bureaucratic barriers.

The Evidence   128 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does public sector teaching hospitals expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.000) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
