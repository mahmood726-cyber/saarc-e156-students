The Evidence   127 words · target 156
What does analysis of public sector teaching hospitals reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that public sector teaching hospitals like mayo? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that public sector teaching hospitals quantifies. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.00 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These findings confirm that jpmc, and lady reading serve the majority of pakistan's population but host a minority of clinical trials due to infrastructure and bureaucratic barriers, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
