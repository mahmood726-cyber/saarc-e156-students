The Evidence   124 words · target 156
What does analysis of academic vs industry ratio reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that the academic-to-industry trial ratio in pakistan reveals a research ecosystem where industry sets the agenda? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that academic vs industry ratio quantifies. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.00 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These findings confirm that leaving gaps in public health research that academic institutions cannot fill alone, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
