The Evidence   128 words · target 156
What does analysis of karachi's global trial share reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that karachi's global trial share relative to its population reveals a city that punches below its demographic weight? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan registered 4,669 trials with the distribution across the karachi's global trial share dimension showing marked inequality. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings confirm that hosting fewer trials per capita than mumbai, bangkok, or cairo despite comparable size, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
