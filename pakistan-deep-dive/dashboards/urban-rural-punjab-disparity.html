Why It Matters

Even within Punjab — Pakistan's most trial-rich province — research concentrates in Lahore while the agricultural heartland of southern Punjab with 50 million people is a research desert.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does urban-rural punjab disparity expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Gini coefficient of trial distribution as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.000) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
