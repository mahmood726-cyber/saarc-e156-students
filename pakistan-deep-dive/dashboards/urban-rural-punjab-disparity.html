Why It Matters

Even within Punjab — Pakistan's most trial-rich province — research concentrates in Lahore while the agricultural heartland of southern Punjab with 50 million people is a research desert.

The Evidence   151 words · target 156
What does analysis of urban-rural punjab disparity reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that even within punjab — pakistan's most trial-rich province — research concentrates in lahore while the agricultural heartland of southern punjab with 50 million people is a research desert.? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that urban-rural punjab disparity quantifies. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.00 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These findings confirm that even within punjab — pakistan's most trial-rich province — research concentrates in lahore while the agricultural heartland of southern punjab with 50 million people is a research desert, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
