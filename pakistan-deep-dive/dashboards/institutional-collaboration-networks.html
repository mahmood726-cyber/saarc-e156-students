What does analysis of institutional collaboration networks reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that network analysis of institutional collaborations reveals a hub-and-spoke model centered on akuh and kemu? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the network degree centrality, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that institutional collaboration networks quantifies. Shannon entropy of -0.00 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These findings confirm that with most institutions operating as isolated research nodes rather than collaborative partners, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
