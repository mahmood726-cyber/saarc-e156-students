What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does hec research funding expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the linear regression slope as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.000) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
