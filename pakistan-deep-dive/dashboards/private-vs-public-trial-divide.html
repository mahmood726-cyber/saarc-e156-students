Why It Matters

Private hospitals host the majority of Pakistan's industry-sponsored trials while public hospitals conduct most academic research, creating two parallel trial ecosystems with different quality standards.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does private vs public trial divide expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.000) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
