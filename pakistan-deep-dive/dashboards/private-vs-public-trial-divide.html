Why It Matters

Private hospitals host the majority of Pakistan's industry-sponsored trials while public hospitals conduct most academic research, creating two parallel trial ecosystems with different quality standards.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
What does analysis of private vs public trial divide reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that private hospitals host the majority of pakistan's industry-sponsored trials while public hospitals conduct most academic research? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan's 4,669 trials showed a Gini of 0.00, confirming severe within-country concentration that private vs public trial divide quantifies. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.00 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These findings confirm that creating two parallel trial ecosystems with different quality standards, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
