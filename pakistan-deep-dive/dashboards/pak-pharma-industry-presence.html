Why It Matters

Pakistan's pharmaceutical industry — 700+ companies — is the largest in SAARC after India, yet local pharma sponsors fewer than 15% of clinical trials, preferring manufacturing over innovation.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does pharma industry presence expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.000, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 10000.000, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
