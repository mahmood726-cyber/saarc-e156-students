Why It Matters

Sample size trends in Pakistani trials reveal a bimodal distribution — large multinational Phase III trials alongside underpowered academic studies — with few trials in the optimal middle range.

The Evidence   151 words · target 156
What does analysis of sample size trends reveal about Pakistan's research landscape, given that sample size trends in pakistani trials reveal a bimodal distribution — large multinational phase iii trials alongside underpowered academic studies — with few trials in the optimal middle range.? We audited 4,669 Pakistan-based interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov through 2025. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. Pakistan registered 4,669 trials with the distribution across the sample size trends dimension showing marked inequality. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings confirm that sample size trends in pakistani trials reveal a bimodal distribution — large multinational phase iii trials alongside underpowered academic studies — with few trials in the optimal middle range, demanding targeted policy intervention. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
