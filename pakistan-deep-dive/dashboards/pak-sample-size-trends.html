Why It Matters

Sample size trends in Pakistani trials reveal a bimodal distribution — large multinational Phase III trials alongside underpowered academic studies — with few trials in the optimal middle range.

The Evidence   122 words · target 156
What does Pakistan's clinical trial landscape reveal about research capacity and equity, and does sample size trends expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,669 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the linear regression slope as the primary estimand using registry metadata. Pakistan registered 4,669 trials across its provinces with concentration in Punjab and Sindh dominating output. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings reveal that Pakistan's research capacity is concentrated in a few urban centres, leaving most provinces without clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
