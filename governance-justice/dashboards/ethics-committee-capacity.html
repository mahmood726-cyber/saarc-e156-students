The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does ethics committee capacity reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that many saarc nations have fewer than 10 accredited ethics committees reviewing hundreds of protocols? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of ethics committee capacity across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These governance patterns reveal that creating bottlenecks that delay research and may compromise review quality, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
