The Evidence   127 words · target 156
What does trial waste index reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that avoidable research waste from poor design? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. The trial waste index analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These governance patterns reveal that incomplete reporting, and non-publication is estimated at 85% globally but may be even higher in saarc where resource constraints amplify the cost of waste, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
