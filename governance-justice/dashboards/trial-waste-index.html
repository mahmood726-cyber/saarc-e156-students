Why It Matters

Avoidable research waste from poor design, incomplete reporting, and non-publication is estimated at 85% globally but may be even higher in SAARC where resource constraints amplify the cost of waste.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does trial waste index expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Gini coefficient of trial distribution as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.711) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
