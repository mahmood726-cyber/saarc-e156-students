Why It Matters

Pediatric trials in SAARC face unique consent challenges: child marriage, child labor, extended family decision-making, and varying age-of-assent norms across countries complicate ethical oversight.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does the distribution of Pediatric trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 1,042 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 1,042 Pediatric trials, with India hosting 33.5% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
