Does clinical trial investment in Pediatric across SAARC nations match regional need, given that pediatric trials in saarc face unique consent challenges: child marriage? This cross-sectional audit examined 1,042 Pediatric trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 1,042 Pediatric trials with Pakistan accounting for 49%, yielding a 9.3-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0021) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These governance patterns reveal that child labor, extended family decision-making, and varying age-of-assent norms across countries complicate ethical oversight, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
