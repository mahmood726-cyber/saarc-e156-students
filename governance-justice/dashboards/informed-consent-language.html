Why It Matters

SAARC nations speak hundreds of languages, yet trial consent documents are typically in English or a single national language, excluding populations whose mother tongue is neither.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does informed consent language reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that saarc nations speak hundreds of languages? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for informed consent language was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These governance patterns reveal that yet trial consent documents are typically in english or a single national language, excluding populations whose mother tongue is neither, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
