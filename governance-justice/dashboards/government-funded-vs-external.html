The Evidence   124 words · target 156
What does government-funded vs external reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that government-funded trials in saarc represent a small fraction of the total? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. The government-funded vs external analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These governance patterns reveal that meaning research agendas are largely set by foreign sponsors whose priorities may not align with national health needs, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
