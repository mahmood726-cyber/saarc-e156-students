The Evidence   122 words · target 156
What does pharma vs academic split reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that the pharma-academic split in saarc trials reveals that industry funds the majority? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. The pharma vs academic split analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These governance patterns reveal that setting research agendas around commercial products rather than public health priorities, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
