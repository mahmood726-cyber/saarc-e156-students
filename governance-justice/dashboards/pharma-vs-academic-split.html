Why It Matters

The pharma-academic split in SAARC trials reveals that industry funds the majority, setting research agendas around commercial products rather than public health priorities.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does pharma vs academic split expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.711) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
