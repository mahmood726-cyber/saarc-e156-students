Why It Matters

Community engagement in SAARC trials ranges from tokenistic CABs to deep participatory models like BRAC's community health worker networks, but formal documentation of engagement methods remains rare.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does community engagement models expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
