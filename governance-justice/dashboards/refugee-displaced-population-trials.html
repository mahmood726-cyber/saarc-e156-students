Why It Matters

SAARC hosts millions of displaced people — Afghan refugees in Pakistan, Rohingya in Bangladesh, Tamil IDPs in Sri Lanka — whose health needs are largely absent from the clinical trial evidence base.

The Evidence   119 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does refugee & displaced population trials expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
