The Evidence   128 words · target 156
What does open access results sharing reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that saarc researchers face a double bind: publish in high-impact paywalled journals for career advancement? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of open access results sharing across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These governance patterns reveal that or choose open access with article processing charges that consume their entire research budget, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
