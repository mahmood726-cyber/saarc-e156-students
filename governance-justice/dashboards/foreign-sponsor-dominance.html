The Evidence   119 words · target 156
What does foreign sponsor dominance reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that foreign pharmaceutical companies sponsor the majority of saarc trials? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index, computed from registry-level metadata. The foreign sponsor dominance analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These governance patterns reveal that using south asian populations to generate evidence for global markets while contributing minimally to local research capacity, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
