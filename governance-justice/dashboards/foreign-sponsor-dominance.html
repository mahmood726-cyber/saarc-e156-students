Why It Matters

Foreign pharmaceutical companies sponsor the majority of SAARC trials, using South Asian populations to generate evidence for global markets while contributing minimally to local research capacity.

The Evidence   113 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does foreign sponsor dominance expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 4164.242, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
