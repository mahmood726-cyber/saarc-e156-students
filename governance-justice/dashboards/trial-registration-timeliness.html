The Evidence   123 words · target 156
What does trial registration timeliness reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that prospective trial registration before enrollment is an icmje requirement that many saarc trials fail to meet? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of trial registration timeliness across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These governance patterns reveal that with retrospective registration rates exceeding 40% in some countries, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
