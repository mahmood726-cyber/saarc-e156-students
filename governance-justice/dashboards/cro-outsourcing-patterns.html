Why It Matters

India is the world's largest CRO outsourcing destination, but this positions South Asian populations as low-cost research subjects for multinational sponsors rather than sovereign research actors.

The Evidence   117 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does cro outsourcing patterns expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 4164.242, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
