Why It Matters

Only a fraction of SAARC trials report results on ClinicalTrials.gov or in peer-reviewed publications, wasting research investment and hiding potentially important safety signals.

The Evidence   119 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does results reporting compliance expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
