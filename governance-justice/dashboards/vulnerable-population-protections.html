The Evidence   119 words · target 156
What does vulnerable population protections reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that refugees? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the odds ratio of trial participation, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of vulnerable population protections across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=0.031, confirming the disparity is statistically significant at the 5% level. These governance patterns reveal that migrant workers, rohingya populations, and other vulnerable groups in saarc face either blanket exclusion from trials or inadequate safeguards when included, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
