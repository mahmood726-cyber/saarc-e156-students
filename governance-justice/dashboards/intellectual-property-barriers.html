The Evidence   122 words · target 156
What does intellectual property barriers reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that trips compliance and patent regimes create barriers to generic drug trials in saarc? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of intellectual property barriers across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0222) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These governance patterns reveal that paradoxically restricting the region that produces the world's most affordable medicines, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
