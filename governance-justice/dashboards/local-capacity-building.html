The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does local capacity building reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that capacity building claims in trial protocols rarely translate into sustained local infrastructure — once the trial ends? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of local capacity building across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These governance patterns reveal that equipment, training, and institutional knowledge frequently dissipate, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
