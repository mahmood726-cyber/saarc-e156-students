The Evidence   120 words · target 156
What does who essential medicines trials reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that the who essential medicines list defines treatments for priority diseases? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for who essential medicines trials was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These governance patterns reveal that yet saarc trial investment only partially aligns with these globally endorsed priorities, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
