Why It Matters

South-South collaboration between SAARC and African or Latin American nations could create alternative research partnerships, but such networks remain embryonic compared to North-South collaborations.

The Evidence   116 words · target 156
Who controls and benefits from clinical research across the SAARC nations, and does south-south collaboration expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the network degree centrality as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 11,116 SAARC trials (48.6%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These findings demonstrate that structural governance deficits perpetuate research dependency and limit South Asian sovereignty over clinical evidence. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
