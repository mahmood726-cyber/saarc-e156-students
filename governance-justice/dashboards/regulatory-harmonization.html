Why It Matters

Each SAARC nation maintains separate regulatory frameworks with no mutual recognition, meaning a trial approved in India must repeat the entire approval process in Pakistan — a barrier that fragments the regional research space.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
What does regulatory harmonization reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that each saarc nation maintains separate regulatory frameworks with no mutual recognition? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the network degree centrality, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for regulatory harmonization was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These governance patterns reveal that meaning a trial approved in india must repeat the entire approval process in pakistan — a barrier that fragments the regional research space, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
