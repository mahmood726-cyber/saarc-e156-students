What does gender in pi leadership reveal about research sovereignty in South Asia, given that female principal investigators lead a small minority of saarc trials despite women comprising 70% of healthcare workers in countries like bangladesh? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the odds ratio of trial participation, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of gender in pi leadership across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These governance patterns reveal that reflecting deeply embedded patriarchal academic structures, threatening research sovereignty. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
