The Evidence   122 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does geographic equity trend expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the linear regression slope as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Theil index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality, with most disparity arising from inter-regional gaps. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
