The Evidence   147 words · target 156
How does geographic equity trend expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that longitudinal analysis of geographic equity across saarc reveals whether the trial distribution is becoming more equitable over time or whether concentration in india continues to intensify.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. The geographic equity trend analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These spatial patterns demonstrate that longitudinal analysis of geographic equity across saarc reveals whether the trial distribution is becoming more equitable over time or whether concentration in india continues to intensify, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
