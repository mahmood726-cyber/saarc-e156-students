The Evidence   117 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does conflict-stability trial gradient expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4,766 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 3 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the linear regression slope as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC registered 4,766 trials across eight nations with severe concentration in a single dominant country. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
