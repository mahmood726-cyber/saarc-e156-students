The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does afghanistan conflict zone trials expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that decades of conflict have devastated afghanistan's research infrastructure? This cross-sectional audit examined 22 interventional trials across 1 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of afghanistan conflict zone trials across 22 SAARC trials revealed Afghanistan hosting 100% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=1.000, confirming the disparity is not statistically significant at the 5% level. These spatial patterns demonstrate that leaving a nation of 40 million with fewer clinical trials than many individual hospitals in india, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
