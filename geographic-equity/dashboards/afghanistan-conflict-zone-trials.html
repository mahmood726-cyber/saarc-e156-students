The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does afghanistan conflict zone trials expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 22 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC registered 22 trials across eight nations with severe concentration in a single dominant country. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
