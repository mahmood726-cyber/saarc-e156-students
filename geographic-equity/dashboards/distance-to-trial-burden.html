Why It Matters

For the average rural South Asian, the nearest clinical trial site may be hundreds of kilometers away — a distance measured not in hours but in days of travel across congested roads and seasonal floodwaters.

The Evidence   132 words · target 156
How does distance-to-trial burden expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that for the average rural south asian? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. The distance-to-trial burden analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that the nearest clinical trial site may be hundreds of kilometers away — a distance measured not in hours but in days of travel across congested roads and seasonal floodwaters, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
