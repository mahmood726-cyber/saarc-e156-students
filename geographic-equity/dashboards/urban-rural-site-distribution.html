The Evidence   130 words · target 156
How does urban-rural site distribution expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that over 65% of south asians live in rural areas? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. The urban-rural site distribution analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that yet trial sites cluster exclusively in urban centers, creating an evidence base derived from populations that differ systematically from the rural majority, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
