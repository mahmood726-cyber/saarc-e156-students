Why It Matters

Capital cities monopolize SAARC clinical trial infrastructure because they host major teaching hospitals, have reliable electricity and internet, and offer transport links that sponsors require for monitoring.

The Evidence   116 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does capital city monopoly index expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 4164.242, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
