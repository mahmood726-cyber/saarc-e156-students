Why It Matters

The Evidence   127 words · target 156
How does site fragmentation index expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that trial site fragmentation across saarc reveals a paradox: india has many sites but they cluster in a few cities? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. The site fragmentation index analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These spatial patterns demonstrate that while smaller nations have too few sites to even measure fragmentation, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
