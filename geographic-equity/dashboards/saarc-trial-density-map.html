Why It Matters

SAARC nations collectively host over 1.9 billion people but clinical trial density varies by orders of magnitude, from India's thousands to Bhutan's near-zero registrations.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does saarc trial density map expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Gini coefficient of trial distribution as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
