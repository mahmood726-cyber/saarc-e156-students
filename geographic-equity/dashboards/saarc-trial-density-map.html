The Evidence   123 words · target 156
How does saarc trial density map expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that saarc nations collectively host over 1.9 billion people but clinical trial density varies by orders of magnitude? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. The saarc trial density map analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These spatial patterns demonstrate that from india's thousands to bhutan's near-zero registrations, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
