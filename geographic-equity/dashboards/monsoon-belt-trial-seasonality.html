Why It Matters

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does monsoon belt trial seasonality expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that the south asian monsoon disrupts trial operations for 3-4 months annually through flooding? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of monsoon belt trial seasonality across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=0.031, confirming the disparity is statistically significant at the 5% level. These spatial patterns demonstrate that transport paralysis, and vector-borne disease surges that confound endpoint assessment, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
