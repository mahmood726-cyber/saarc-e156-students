How does sri lanka punch-above-weight expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that sri lanka's universal healthcare system and high literacy rate create a research-ready population that punches above its demographic weight in clinical trial participation.? This cross-sectional audit examined 75 interventional trials across 1 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of sri lanka punch-above-weight across 75 SAARC trials revealed Sri Lanka hosting 100% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=1.000, confirming the disparity is not statistically significant at the 5% level. These spatial patterns demonstrate that sri lanka's universal healthcare system and high literacy rate create a research-ready population that punches above its demographic weight in clinical trial participation, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
