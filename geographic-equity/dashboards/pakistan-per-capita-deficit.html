Why It Matters

The Evidence   129 words · target 156
How does pakistan per-capita deficit expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that pakistan's 230 million people make it the fifth most populous nation? This cross-sectional audit examined 4,669 interventional trials across 1 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of pakistan per-capita deficit across 4,669 SAARC trials revealed Pakistan hosting 100% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0093) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These spatial patterns demonstrate that yet its per-capita clinical trial rate is a fraction of india's, reflecting chronic underinvestment in research infrastructure, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
