The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does delta & floodplain populations expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 6,050 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 2 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. India hosted 5,398 of 6,050 SAARC trials (89.2%), leaving the remaining seven nations with marginal research portfolios. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
