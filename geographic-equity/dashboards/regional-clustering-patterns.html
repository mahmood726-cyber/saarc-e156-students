The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How does regional clustering patterns expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that spatial clustering analysis reveals that saarc trials form distinct geographic clusters around major medical centers? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Moran's I spatial autocorrelation, computed from registry-level metadata. The regional clustering patterns HHI was 4,164.2, exceeding the concentration threshold, with India dominating at 49%. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These spatial patterns demonstrate that with vast intervening territories that are research deserts, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
