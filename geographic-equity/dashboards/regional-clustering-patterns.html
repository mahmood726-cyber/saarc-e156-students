The Evidence   120 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does regional clustering patterns expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Moran's I spatial autocorrelation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index was 4164.242, exceeding the 0.25 threshold and indicating a highly concentrated regional trial portfolio. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.48 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
