How does bangladesh growth trajectory expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that bangladesh's clinical trial portfolio has grown steadily? This cross-sectional audit examined 652 interventional trials across 1 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of bangladesh growth trajectory across 652 SAARC trials revealed Bangladesh hosting 100% of the regional portfolio. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0013) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These spatial patterns demonstrate that driven by icddr,b and brac health networks, but the trajectory remains insufficient relative to its 170 million population and disease burden, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
