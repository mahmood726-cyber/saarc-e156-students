Why It Matters

The rural reach coefficient measures the fraction of trials conducted outside major urban centers, revealing that less than 5% of SAARC trials reach the rural communities where the majority of people live.

The Evidence   131 words · target 156
How does rural reach coefficient expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that the rural reach coefficient measures the fraction of trials conducted outside major urban centers? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Gini coefficient of trial distribution, computed from registry-level metadata. The rural reach coefficient analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that revealing that less than 5% of saarc trials reach the rural communities where the majority of people live, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
