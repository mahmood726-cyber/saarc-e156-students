The Evidence   127 words · target 156
How does diaspora return research expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that south asian diaspora researchers in the us? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the network degree centrality, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of diaspora return research across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These spatial patterns demonstrate that uk, and gulf states increasingly serve as bridge figures connecting saarc trial sites with global networks, but their role can perpetuate dependency, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
