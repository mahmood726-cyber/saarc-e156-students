The Evidence   121 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does bhutan micro-state research expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 6 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 1 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC registered 6 trials across eight nations with severe concentration in a single dominant country. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was -0.00 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
