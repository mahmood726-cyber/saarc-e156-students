The Evidence   126 words · target 156
How does regional health expenditure match expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that health expenditure per capita ranges from $12 in afghanistan to $200+ in maldives? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Spearman rank correlation, computed from registry-level metadata. The regional health expenditure match analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that and trial density tracks spending rather than disease burden, creating a wealth-research feedback loop, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
