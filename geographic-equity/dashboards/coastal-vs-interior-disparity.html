The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does coastal vs interior disparity expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that coastal cities like mumbai? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of coastal vs interior disparity across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=0.031, confirming the disparity is statistically significant at the 5% level. These spatial patterns demonstrate that karachi, and colombo dominate trial infrastructure due to port logistics, international connectivity, and colonial-era investment patterns that persist today, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
