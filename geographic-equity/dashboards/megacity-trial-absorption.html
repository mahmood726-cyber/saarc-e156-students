Why It Matters

Megacities like Delhi, Mumbai, Dhaka, and Karachi absorb the vast majority of their nation's trials, creating urban research enclaves that bear little resemblance to the populations they claim to serve.

The Evidence   115 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does megacity trial absorption expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,116 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.711, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 4164.242, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
