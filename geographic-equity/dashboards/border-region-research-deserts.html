Why It Matters

Border regions between SAARC nations are doubly disadvantaged — too remote for national research networks and politically sensitive for cross-border collaboration — creating some of the world's largest research deserts.

The Evidence   119 words · target 156
How equitably are clinical trials distributed across South Asia's eight SAARC nations, and does border region research deserts expose structural inequity in South Asian research? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 11,110 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Moran's I spatial autocorrelation as the primary estimand using registry metadata. The distribution yielded a Gini coefficient of 0.712, indicating severe concentration of trials among few nations. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.712) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These findings reveal a geographic research monopoly where most SAARC nations remain functionally invisible in the clinical evidence landscape. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
