The Evidence   118 words · target 156
How does economic corridor alignment expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that cpec? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Spearman rank correlation, computed from registry-level metadata. The economic corridor alignment analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that the golden quadrilateral, and other economic corridors shape trial site geography by determining where infrastructure, hospitals, and transport networks concentrate, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
