The Evidence   133 words · target 156
How does sez & medical tourism sites expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that special economic zones and medical tourism hubs in india and sri lanka attract clinical trials through tax incentives and world-class hospital infrastructure? This cross-sectional audit examined 5,473 interventional trials across 2 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of sez & medical tourism sites across 5,473 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 99% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These spatial patterns demonstrate that further concentrating research in privileged enclaves, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
