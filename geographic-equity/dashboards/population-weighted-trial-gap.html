Why It Matters

When trials are weighted by population, Pakistan's 230 million people face the largest absolute research deficit in SAARC, followed by Bangladesh's 170 million.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
How does population-weighted trial gap expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that when trials are weighted by population? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of population-weighted trial gap across 11,116 SAARC trials revealed India hosting 49% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These spatial patterns demonstrate that pakistan's 230 million people face the largest absolute research deficit in saarc, followed by bangladesh's 170 million, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
