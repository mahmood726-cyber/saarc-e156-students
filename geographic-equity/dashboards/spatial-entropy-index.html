The Evidence   147 words · target 156
How does spatial entropy index expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that shannon entropy applied to trial site distribution reveals extremely low spatial randomness — trial placement is driven by institutional convenience rather than population health need.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Shannon entropy of trial allocation, computed from registry-level metadata. The spatial entropy index analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Theil decomposition index of 0.918 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These spatial patterns demonstrate that shannon entropy applied to trial site distribution reveals extremely low spatial randomness — trial placement is driven by institutional convenience rather than population health need, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
