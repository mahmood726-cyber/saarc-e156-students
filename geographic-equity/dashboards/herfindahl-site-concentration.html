How does herfindahl site concentration expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that the herfindahl-hirschman index for trial site concentration across saarc reveals monopolistic patterns that would trigger antitrust scrutiny in commercial markets.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index, computed from registry-level metadata. The herfindahl site concentration analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These spatial patterns demonstrate that the herfindahl-hirschman index for trial site concentration across saarc reveals monopolistic patterns that would trigger antitrust scrutiny in commercial markets, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
