The Evidence   129 words · target 156
How does altitude & access barriers expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that high-altitude populations across the himalayas? This cross-sectional audit examined 4,969 interventional trials across 3 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Moran's I spatial autocorrelation, computed from registry-level metadata. The altitude & access barriers analysis yielded a Gini of 0.63, with Pakistan hosting 94% of 4,969 trials. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.63 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These spatial patterns demonstrate that karakoram, and hindu kush have unique health profiles — altitude sickness, uv exposure, cold-related illness — yet are entirely absent from clinical trial enrollment, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
