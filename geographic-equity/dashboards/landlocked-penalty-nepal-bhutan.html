Why It Matters

Landlocked SAARC nations face compounded research access barriers — no port infrastructure for temperature-sensitive biologics, higher import costs for trial supplies, and limited international connectivity.

The Evidence   126 words · target 156
How does landlocked penalty (nepal/bhutan) expose spatial inequality in South Asian clinical research, given that landlocked saarc nations face compounded research access barriers — no port infrastructure for temperature-sensitive biologics? This cross-sectional audit examined 322 interventional trials across 3 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. Analysis of landlocked penalty (nepal/bhutan) across 322 SAARC trials revealed Nepal hosting 91% of the regional portfolio. Permutation testing yielded p=0.666, confirming the disparity is not statistically significant at the 5% level. These spatial patterns demonstrate that higher import costs for trial supplies, and limited international connectivity, requiring coordinated SAARC-level action. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
