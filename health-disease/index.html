Cardiovascular Trial Gap

Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Cardiovascular Diseases trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 1,024 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 1,024 Cardiovascular Diseases trials, with India hosting 50.1% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.713) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia.
