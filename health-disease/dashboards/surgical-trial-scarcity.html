Why It Matters

South Asia faces a surgical workforce crisis with 0.7 surgeons per 100,000 versus 50 in Europe, yet surgical trial investment is among the lowest of all specialties in the region.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Surgery trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 567 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 567 Surgery trials, with India hosting 49.0% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
