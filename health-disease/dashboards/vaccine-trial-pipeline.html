Why It Matters

India's Serum Institute produces more vaccine doses than any manufacturer globally, yet vaccine trial design in SAARC rarely addresses the unique immunological profiles of South Asian populations.

The Evidence   113 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Vaccine trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 253 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 253 Vaccine trials, with India hosting 47.8% of the regional portfolio. The Herfindahl-Hirschman index reached 3631.833, corroborating the inequality finding across SAARC nations. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
