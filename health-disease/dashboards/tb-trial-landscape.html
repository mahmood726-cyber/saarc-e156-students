The Evidence   124 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Tuberculosis across SAARC nations match regional need, given that saarc nations carry one-third of the global tb burden? This cross-sectional audit examined 95 Tuberculosis trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Bayesian posterior trial rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 95 Tuberculosis trials with India accounting for 65%, yielding a 2.5-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0002) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These results underscore that with india, pakistan, and bangladesh among the top 8 high-burden countries, yet the drug-resistant tb pipeline remains critically thin, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
