Why It Matters

The Evidence   117 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Tuberculosis trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 95 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Bayesian posterior trial rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 95 Tuberculosis trials, with India hosting 65.3% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
