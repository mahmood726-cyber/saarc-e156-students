Why It Matters

The Evidence   123 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Air Pollution across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia has 14 of the world's 20 most polluted cities? This cross-sectional audit examined 258 Air Pollution trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Spearman rank correlation, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 258 Air Pollution trials with India accounting for 50%, yielding a 5.6-fold per-capita disparity. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results underscore that yet respiratory trial investment barely acknowledges the unique phenotype of pollution-driven copd in never-smokers, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
