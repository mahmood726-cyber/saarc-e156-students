Why It Matters

Pneumonia and diarrhea together kill more SAARC children than any other causes, yet the trial pipeline for improved oral rehydration, zinc, and pneumococcal vaccines in South Asian populations is thin.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Pneumonia trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 421 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 421 Pneumonia trials, with India hosting 40.9% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
