Why It Matters

Pneumonia and diarrhea together kill more SAARC children than any other causes, yet the trial pipeline for improved oral rehydration, zinc, and pneumococcal vaccines in South Asian populations is thin.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Pneumonia across SAARC nations match regional need, given that pneumonia and diarrhea together kill more saarc children than any other causes? This cross-sectional audit examined 421 Pneumonia trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 421 Pneumonia trials with India accounting for 41%, yielding a 4.7-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0008) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These results underscore that yet the trial pipeline for improved oral rehydration, zinc, and pneumococcal vaccines in south asian populations is thin, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
