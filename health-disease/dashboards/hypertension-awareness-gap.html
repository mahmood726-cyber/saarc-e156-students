Why It Matters

With 30-40% prevalence and under 25% awareness, hypertension is South Asia's silent killer — yet community-based screening and treatment trial designs remain rare.

The Evidence   117 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Hypertension across SAARC nations match regional need, given that with 30-40% prevalence and under 25% awareness? This cross-sectional audit examined 253 Hypertension trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 253 Hypertension trials with India accounting for 53%, yielding a 3.8-fold per-capita disparity. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.70 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These results underscore that hypertension is south asia's silent killer — yet community-based screening and treatment trial designs remain rare, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
