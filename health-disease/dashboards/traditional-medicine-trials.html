Why It Matters

Ayurveda, Unani, and Siddha systems serve hundreds of millions of South Asians, yet rigorous clinical trials of traditional remedies remain rare despite government initiatives like India's AYUSH ministry.

The Evidence   122 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Ayurveda trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 15 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 15 Ayurveda trials, with India hosting 53.3% of the regional portfolio. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was 1.53 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
