Why It Matters

Ayurveda, Unani, and Siddha systems serve hundreds of millions of South Asians, yet rigorous clinical trials of traditional remedies remain rare despite government initiatives like India's AYUSH ministry.

The Evidence   124 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Ayurveda across SAARC nations match regional need, given that ayurveda? This cross-sectional audit examined 15 Ayurveda trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 15 Ayurveda trials with India accounting for 53%, yielding a 4.0-fold per-capita disparity. Shannon entropy of 1.53 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These results underscore that unani, and siddha systems serve hundreds of millions of south asians, yet rigorous clinical trials of traditional remedies remain rare despite government initiatives like india's ayush ministry, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
