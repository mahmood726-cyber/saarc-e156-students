The Evidence   127 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Occupational Health across SAARC nations match regional need, given that textile workers in bangladesh? This cross-sectional audit examined 13 Occupational Health trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 13 Occupational Health trials with India accounting for 38%, yielding a 3.8-fold per-capita disparity. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results underscore that brick kiln laborers in pakistan, and pesticide-exposed farmers across saarc face occupational hazards with virtually no trial evidence for prevention or treatment, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
