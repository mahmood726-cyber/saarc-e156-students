Why It Matters

Burns affect 300,000 South Asians annually — many from domestic cooking fires and acid attacks — yet burn care trial investment is negligible despite high mortality and disability burden.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Burns across SAARC nations match regional need, given that burns affect 300? This cross-sectional audit examined 520 Burns trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 520 Burns trials with Pakistan accounting for 65%, yielding a 14.8-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0010) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These results underscore that 000 south asians annually — many from domestic cooking fires and acid attacks — yet burn care trial investment is negligible despite high mortality and disability burden, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
