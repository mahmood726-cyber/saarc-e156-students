Why It Matters

Burns affect 300,000 South Asians annually — many from domestic cooking fires and acid attacks — yet burn care trial investment is negligible despite high mortality and disability burden.

The Evidence   116 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Burns trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 520 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 520 Burns trials, with India hosting 28.1% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
