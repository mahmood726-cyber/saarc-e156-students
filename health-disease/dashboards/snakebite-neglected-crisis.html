Why It Matters

India alone accounts for nearly half of global snakebite deaths, yet antivenom trial investment is near zero and the subcontinent relies on antivenoms with limited cross-species efficacy.

The Evidence   116 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Snakebite trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 7 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 7 Snakebite trials, with India hosting 57.1% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
