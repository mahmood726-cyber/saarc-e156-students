The Evidence   151 words · target 156
Does disease burden concordance index reveal a mismatch between disease burden and trial investment across SAARC, given that the concordance between disease burden (dalys) and trial investment across saarc reveals systematic misalignment — conditions causing the most suffering receive disproportionately little research attention.? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Spearman rank correlation, computed from registry-level metadata. The disease burden concordance index analysis yielded a Gini of 0.71, with India hosting 49% of 11,116 trials. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These results underscore that the concordance between disease burden (dalys) and trial investment across saarc reveals systematic misalignment — conditions causing the most suffering receive disproportionately little research attention, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
