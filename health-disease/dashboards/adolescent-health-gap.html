Why It Matters

South Asia has 350 million adolescents whose health needs — mental health, sexual health, nutrition — are poorly addressed by trial designs that treat them as either small adults or large children.

The Evidence   133 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Adolescent Health across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia has 350 million adolescents whose health needs — mental health? This cross-sectional audit examined 208 Adolescent Health trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 208 Adolescent Health trials with India accounting for 63%, yielding a 2.3-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0004) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These results underscore that sexual health, nutrition — are poorly addressed by trial designs that treat them as either small adults or large children, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
