Why It Matters

South Asia has 350 million adolescents whose health needs — mental health, sexual health, nutrition — are poorly addressed by trial designs that treat them as either small adults or large children.

The Evidence   123 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Adolescent Health trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 208 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 208 Adolescent Health trials, with India hosting 63.5% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
