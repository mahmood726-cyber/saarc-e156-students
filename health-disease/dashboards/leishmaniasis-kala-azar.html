Why It Matters

The India-Nepal-Bangladesh triangle is the world's largest visceral leishmaniasis endemic zone, and elimination efforts depend on a trial pipeline for short-course, oral treatments.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Leishmaniasis trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 39 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 39 Leishmaniasis trials, with India hosting 51.3% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
