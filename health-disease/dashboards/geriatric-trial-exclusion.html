Why It Matters

South Asia's over-60 population will reach 300 million by 2050, yet geriatric medicine trials are almost nonexistent — the region is ageing into an evidence vacuum with no adapted treatment protocols.

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Elderly trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 212 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 212 Elderly trials, with India hosting 42.5% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
