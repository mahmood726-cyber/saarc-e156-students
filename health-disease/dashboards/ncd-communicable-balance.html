Why It Matters

South Asia faces a dual burden — communicable diseases that haven't been conquered and NCDs that are arriving early — yet the trial portfolio tilts heavily toward infectious disease, leaving the NCD epidemic under-researched.

The Evidence   136 words · target 156
Does ncd vs communicable balance reveal a mismatch between disease burden and trial investment across SAARC, given that south asia faces a dual burden — communicable diseases that haven't been conquered and ncds that are arriving early — yet the trial portfolio tilts heavily toward infectious disease? This cross-sectional audit examined 11,116 interventional trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the chi-squared test of distributional uniformity, computed from registry-level metadata. Shannon entropy for ncd vs communicable balance was 1.48 bits across 11,116 trials, indicating low distributional evenness. Shannon entropy of 1.48 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These results underscore that leaving the ncd epidemic under-researched, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
