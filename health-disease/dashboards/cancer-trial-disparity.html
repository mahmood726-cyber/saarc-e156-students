Why It Matters

Cancer incidence in South Asia is rising rapidly with the epidemiological transition, yet trial investment concentrates on cancers prevalent in Western populations rather than oral, cervical, and gastric cancers dominant in SAARC.

The Evidence   122 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Cancer across SAARC nations match regional need, given that cancer incidence in south asia is rising rapidly with the epidemiological transition? This cross-sectional audit examined 1,047 Cancer trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Herfindahl-Hirschman concentration index, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC registered 1,047 Cancer trials, India hosting 82% while smaller nations contributed minimally. Theil decomposition index of 1.165 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These results underscore that yet trial investment concentrates on cancers prevalent in western populations rather than oral, cervical, and gastric cancers dominant in saarc, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
