Why It Matters

The Evidence   127 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Maternal Health across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia accounts for one-third of global maternal deaths? This cross-sectional audit examined 122 Maternal Health trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 122 Maternal Health trials with Pakistan accounting for 44%, yielding a 9.0-fold per-capita disparity. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.65 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These results underscore that with afghanistan's maternal mortality ratio exceeding 600 per 100,000 — yet obstetric trial infrastructure barely exists outside india, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
