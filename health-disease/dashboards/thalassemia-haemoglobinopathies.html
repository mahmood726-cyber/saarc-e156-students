The Evidence   123 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Thalassemia across SAARC nations match regional need, given that the thalassemia belt runs through south asia? This cross-sectional audit examined 32 Thalassemia trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 32 Thalassemia trials with India accounting for 47%, yielding a 5.9-fold per-capita disparity. Theil decomposition index of 0.388 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These results underscore that with pakistan alone having 100,000 transfusion-dependent patients, yet gene therapy trials concentrate in europe and the us where patients are fewer, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
