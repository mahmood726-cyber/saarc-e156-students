Why It Matters

South Asia accounts for 40% of global neonatal deaths, yet the trial pipeline for context-appropriate low-resource neonatal interventions — kangaroo care, chlorhexidine, simplified antibiotic regimens — remains insufficient.

The Evidence   120 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Neonatal trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 519 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 519 Neonatal trials, with India hosting 48.9% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using Gini coefficient (0.699) confirmed the inequality finding with stable bootstrap estimates. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
