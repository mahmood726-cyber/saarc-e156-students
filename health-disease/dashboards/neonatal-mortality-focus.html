Why It Matters

South Asia accounts for 40% of global neonatal deaths, yet the trial pipeline for context-appropriate low-resource neonatal interventions — kangaroo care, chlorhexidine, simplified antibiotic regimens — remains insufficient.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Neonatal across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia accounts for 40% of global neonatal deaths? This cross-sectional audit examined 519 Neonatal trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 519 Neonatal trials with India accounting for 49%, yielding a 4.9-fold per-capita disparity. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.70 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These results underscore that yet the trial pipeline for context-appropriate low-resource neonatal interventions — kangaroo care, chlorhexidine, simplified antibiotic regimens — remains insufficient, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the registry.
