The Evidence   126 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Palliative Care across SAARC nations match regional need, given that morphine availability in south asia is among the world's lowest due to restrictive narcotics policies? This cross-sectional audit examined 19 Palliative Care trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 19 Palliative Care trials with India accounting for 74%, yielding a 1.4-fold per-capita disparity. Permutation testing yielded p=0.031, confirming the disparity is statistically significant at the 5% level. These results underscore that and the trial pipeline for affordable pain management in the region is nearly empty, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
