Why It Matters

Morphine availability in South Asia is among the world's lowest due to restrictive narcotics policies, and the trial pipeline for affordable pain management in the region is nearly empty.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Palliative Care trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 19 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 19 Palliative Care trials, with India hosting 73.7% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
