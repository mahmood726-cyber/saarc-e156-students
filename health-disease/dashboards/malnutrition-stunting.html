Why It Matters

South Asia has the world's highest stunting prevalence — 35% of children under five — yet nutrition intervention trials rarely account for the region's unique dietary patterns and micronutrient profiles.

The Evidence   155 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Malnutrition across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia has the world's highest stunting prevalence — 35% of children under five — yet nutrition intervention trials rarely account for the region's unique dietary patterns and micronutrient profiles.? This cross-sectional audit examined 205 Malnutrition trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the linear regression slope, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 205 Malnutrition trials with Bangladesh accounting for 39%, yielding a 7.9-fold per-capita disparity. Sensitivity analyses using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results underscore that south asia has the world's highest stunting prevalence — 35% of children under five — yet nutrition intervention trials rarely account for the region's unique dietary patterns and micronutrient profiles, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
