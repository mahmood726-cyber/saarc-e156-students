Why It Matters

South Asia is a global AMR hotspot, with over-the-counter antibiotic access, unregulated veterinary use, and pharmaceutical effluent creating resistance genes that spread globally.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Antimicrobial Resistance trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 10 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 10 Antimicrobial Resistance trials, with India hosting 50.0% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
