The Evidence   130 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Cardiovascular Diseases across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia has the world's highest cardiovascular mortality rate? This cross-sectional audit examined 1,024 Cardiovascular Diseases trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the rate ratio comparing per-capita trial density, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 1,024 Cardiovascular Diseases trials with India accounting for 50%, yielding a 5.2-fold per-capita disparity. Bootstrap sensitivity analysis confirmed the Gini of 0.71 with stable estimates across 1,000 resamples. These results underscore that with events occurring a decade earlier than in europe, yet cv trial investment is a fraction of what the epidemic demands, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
