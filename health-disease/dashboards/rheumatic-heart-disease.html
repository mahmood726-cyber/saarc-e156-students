Why It Matters

Rheumatic heart disease kills more young South Asians than any other cardiac condition and is entirely preventable with penicillin prophylaxis, yet delivery strategy trials are almost nonexistent.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Rheumatic Heart Disease trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 12 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 12 Rheumatic Heart Disease trials, with India hosting 25.0% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
