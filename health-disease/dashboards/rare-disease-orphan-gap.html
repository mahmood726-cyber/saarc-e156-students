Why It Matters

The Evidence   122 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Rare Disease across SAARC nations match regional need, given that south asia's consanguinity rates produce a high burden of rare genetic diseases? This cross-sectional audit examined 4 Rare Disease trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC registered 4 Rare Disease trials, India hosting 100% while smaller nations contributed minimally. Shannon entropy of -0.00 bits corroborated the concentration finding, well below the maximum-evenness benchmark. These results underscore that yet orphan drug development and rare disease trial infrastructure is effectively nonexistent in the region, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
