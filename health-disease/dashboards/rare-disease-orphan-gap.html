Why It Matters

South Asia's consanguinity rates produce a high burden of rare genetic diseases, yet orphan drug development and rare disease trial infrastructure is effectively nonexistent in the region.

The Evidence   118 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Rare Disease trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 4 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Poisson incidence rate as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 4 Rare Disease trials, with India hosting 100.0% of the regional portfolio. Shannon entropy of the trial distribution was -0.00 bits, confirming substantial concentration beyond random variation. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is constrained by missing sub-national data and the exclusion of observational studies from the analysis.
