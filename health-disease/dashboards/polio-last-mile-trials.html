Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Polio trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 12 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 2 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the Kaplan-Meier cumulative registration curve as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 12 Polio trials, with India hosting 0.0% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia.
