Why It Matters

Oral cancer rates in South Asia are among the world's highest due to betel nut, paan, and gutka use, yet oral health trial investment is nearly nonexistent.

The Evidence   125 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Oral Cancer across SAARC nations match regional need, given that oral cancer rates in south asia are among the world's highest due to betel nut? This cross-sectional audit examined 237 Oral Cancer trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Poisson incidence rate, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 237 Oral Cancer trials with India accounting for 59%, yielding a 3.9-fold per-capita disparity. Bayesian rate estimation (posterior mean 0.0005) confirmed the direction and magnitude of the primary finding. These results underscore that paan, and gutka use, yet oral health trial investment is nearly nonexistent, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by single-registry scope and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
