Why It Matters

The Evidence   121 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in Contraception across SAARC nations match regional need, given that unmet need for family planning affects 80 million south asian women? This cross-sectional audit examined 42 Contraception trials across 8 SAARC nations on ClinicalTrials.gov. The primary estimand was the Theil inequality index, computed from registry-level metadata. SAARC hosted 42 Contraception trials with India accounting for 33%, yielding a 5.5-fold per-capita disparity. Theil decomposition index of 0.324 confirmed between-country inequality dominates the overall disparity. These results underscore that yet contraception trial investment focuses on new methods rather than delivery strategies for existing ones in conservative settings, highlighting a critical evidence gap. This analysis is limited by reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov alone, which may undercount locally registered South Asian studies.
