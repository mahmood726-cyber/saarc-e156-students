Why It Matters

CKD prevalence in South Asia is 15-17%, driven by diabetes and hypertension epidemics, yet renal trial investment is negligible and dialysis access in Pakistan and Bangladesh is under 10%.

The Evidence   129 words · target 156
Does clinical trial investment in South Asia match the region's disease burden, and does the distribution of Chronic Kidney Disease trials reveal a systematic research gap? This cross-sectional audit evaluated 95 South Asian trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov across 8 SAARC nations. Investigators computed the rate ratio comparing SAARC nations to global norms as the primary estimand using registry metadata. SAARC nations registered 95 Chronic Kidney Disease trials, with India hosting 80.0% of the regional portfolio. Sensitivity analysis using alternative inequality metrics confirmed the primary finding with consistent direction and magnitude. These results expose a fundamental mismatch between where disease burden falls and where research investment flows across South Asia. Interpretation is limited by the use of a single registry and the absence of national trial databases from SAARC nations.
